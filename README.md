# ⚙️ Cardan / Universal Joint (U-Joint) - 3D Assembly & Motion Study

An industrial-grade parametric 3D mechanical assembly and motion study of a Universal Joint (Cardan Shaft). This project demonstrates complex multi-axial rotational mates, dynamic physical simulations, and precise component clearance modeling using SolidWorks.

## 📐 Engineering & Assembly Specifications

* **Assembly Type:** Rotary Power Transmission Joint.
* **Angular Capability:** Designed to operate at varying shaft angles while transmitting torque seamlessly.
* **Key Components:**
  * **Driving & Driven Yokes (Driving & Driven Shafts):** Heavy-duty forged steel design with keys slots for torque input/output.
  * **Central Cross (Spider):** Precision-machined journal bearing core that allows multi-axial swiveling.
  * **Bushings & Retaining Pins:** Modeled with perfect tolerance constraints to ensure zero radial play.

---

## 🛠️ SolidWorks Features & Animation Skills Demonstrated

* **Advanced Mechanical Mates:** Utilizing concentric, coincident, and advanced mechanical mates to achieve realistic, constraint-free physical rotation between the yokes and the central spider.
* **SolidWorks Motion Analysis:**
  * Configured a rotary motor on the input shaft to analyze rotational torque.
  * Handled contact tracking and collision detection to ensure no component interference during continuous rotation.
* **Animation & Rendering:** * Exported high-quality animation frames from the SolidWorks Motion Study.
  * Converted into a web-optimized `.gif` running smoothly at a high frame rate.
